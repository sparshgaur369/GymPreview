Evolution of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science in Engineering (Morning Session) - Conceptual

The conceptual session on the evolution of artificial intelligence (AI) and data science in engineering delves into the transformative impact these technologies have had on the field. This session outlines the historical development, key milestones, and significant breakthroughs that have shaped AI and data science. It covers foundational concepts, from early machine learning algorithms to advanced neural networks and deep learning models.


Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) for Disaster Management & Precision Agriculture

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), commonly known as drones, have become indispensable tools in both disaster management and precision agriculture. In disaster management, UAVs provide rapid aerial assessments of affected areas, allowing for real-time data collection and high-resolution imaging. This capability helps in locating survivors, assessing damage, and coordinating rescue operations with greater efficiency and accuracy. Drones can access areas that are otherwise unreachable, ensuring timely and effective responses to natural disasters such as floods, earthquakes, and hurricanes. In precision agriculture, UAVs are revolutionizing the way farmers monitor and manage their crops. Equipped with advanced sensors and imaging technologies, drones can collect detailed data on crop health, soil conditions, and water usage. This information enables farmers to make data-driven decisions, optimizing the use of resources like water, fertilizers, and pesticides. UAVs help in identifying pest infestations, disease outbreaks, and areas of underperformance, allowing for targeted interventions that improve crop yields and sustainability.

Hands-on IoT and Its Applications

The "Hands-on IoT and Its Applications" session provides practical experience in the Internet of Things (IoT), a technology paradigm that connects everyday objects to the internet, enabling them to send and receive data. Participants learn about the fundamentals of IoT, including sensor integration, data collection, and communication protocols.

Hands-on Bibliometrics Analysis

The "Hands-on Bibliometrics Analysis" session offers practical training in bibliometrics, the quantitative analysis of academic literature. Participants learn to use various tools and techniques to analyze research outputs, such as scientific publications and citations, to uncover patterns and trends within scholarly communications.

Hands-on Generative AI Techniques

The "Hands-on Generative AI Techniques" session provides participants with practical experience in utilizing generative artificial intelligence to create and manipulate data. This workshop focuses on advanced AI models such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), and transformer-based models, which are capable of generating new content based on learned patterns from existing datasets.

Distributed ML Modelling Using Spark

The "Distributed ML Modelling Using Spark" session offers a comprehensive introduction to building and deploying machine learning models using Apache Spark, a powerful framework for distributed computing. Spark's distributed processing capabilities enable the handling of large-scale datasets efficiently, making it a popular choice for machine learning tasks that require substantial computational resources.

Dataset Acquisition and Deep Learning for Healthcare Analytics (Evening Session) - Lab Handouts

The "Dataset Acquisition and Deep Learning for Healthcare Analytics" lab session offers participants a hands-on opportunity to delve into the intersection of healthcare analytics and deep learning. This session provides lab handouts containing curated datasets representative of healthcare scenarios, along with accompanying instructions for data acquisition, preprocessing, and model development.

Artificial Intelligence for Antenna Design

The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) techniques in antenna design represents a significant advancement in the field of wireless communication technology. Traditionally, antenna design has relied heavily on manual optimization and simulation-based approaches, which can be time-consuming and labor-intensive.
